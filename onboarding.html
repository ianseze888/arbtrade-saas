Wholesale sourcing — step by step
Follow these steps after getting a BUY lead from your ARBTRADE dashboard
1
Verify the lead on Amazon
Before contacting any supplier, confirm the product is still a good opportunity. Check the current Amazon price, seller count, and BSR. Make sure the buy box is rotating between sellers (not dominated by Amazon itself).
2
Find the supplier contact
Your lead includes a suggested source (Faire, RangeMe, Wholesale Central). Click below to find the brand's wholesale contact. Look for a "Wholesale" or "Trade" link on the brand's website, or search the distributor platforms directly.
3
Send account opening email
Use the pre-written email below to contact the supplier. Personalize your business name and store details. Professional outreach dramatically increases your approval rate.
Subject: Wholesale Account Application — [Your Business Name]
4
Get approved and place your first order
Most suppliers respond within 2-5 business days. Once approved, start with a small test order — typically $300-500 minimum. This lets you verify the product quality and sales velocity before scaling up.
5
Ship to your 3PL for FBA prep
Give your supplier your 3PL's address as the ship-to location. Your 3PL will receive the inventory, count it, apply FNSKU labels, poly bag if needed, and ship to Amazon's fulfillment center. Typical 3PL turnaround is 2-3 business days.
6
Go live on Amazon! 🎉
Once Amazon receives your inventory it goes live within 1-3 business days. Monitor your listing for the first 2 weeks — check buy box ownership, pricing, and early sales velocity. Set a reorder reminder for when you hit 30 days of stock remaining.
Online arbitrage — step by step
The fastest path from lead to sales. No supplier relationships needed.
1
Verify the deal is still live
OA deals move fast. Before buying, confirm the sale price is still active on the source website AND verify the current Amazon selling price hasn't dropped. Check that you're not restricted from selling this product.
2
Check if you can sell it
In Seller Central go to Add a Product, search the ASIN, and confirm you're not restricted. Some brands and categories require approval. If restricted, skip this lead and move to the next one.
3
Buy the product
Purchase from the source identified in your lead (Walgreens, CVS, iHerb, etc.). Buy enough units to make the prep and shipping fees worth it — typically 10-20 units minimum. Use cashback apps like Rakuten for extra margin.
4
Ship to your 3PL or prep yourself
For OA you have two options — ship directly to your 3PL for FNSKU labeling and FBA prep, or prep it yourself at home (label, poly bag, box). Self-prep saves money when starting out. 3PL makes sense when volume grows.
5
Create FBA inbound shipment
In Seller Central create an inbound shipment for your products. Amazon will assign a fulfillment center. Print the shipping labels and attach them to your boxes before sending.
6
Go live and monitor! 🎉
Amazon receives your inventory and you go live within 1-3 days. Monitor the buy box and pricing. If the deal was replenishable, set an alert to buy again when stock runs low. OA is all about finding deals that repeat.

Self-Prep Checklist
What you need to prep OA products at home
☐ Print FNSKU labels from Seller Central
☐ Apply FNSKU label over existing barcode
☐ Poly bag items if required (check Amazon guidelines)
☐ Remove or cover any retail pricing stickers
☐ Check expiration dates — Amazon requires 90+ days
☐ Count and verify units match your shipment plan
☐ Box items with appropriate padding
☐ Apply Amazon shipment label to outside of box
☐ Drop off at UPS/FedEx store
